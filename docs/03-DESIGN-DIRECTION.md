# 03 — Design Direction

## Design objective
Make the site feel more polished, cohesive, and engaging without losing clarity or credibility.

## Desired qualities
- Clean
- Deliberate
- Modern
- Premium
- Trustworthy
- Technically competent
- More visually interesting than a stock template

## UX priorities
- Stronger hierarchy
- Better section-to-section flow
- Better scan patterns
- Better spacing rhythm
- Better CTA placement
- Better mobile behavior
- Lower cognitive load

## Visual priorities
- Stronger use of contrast and grouping
- More intentional cards, panels, bands, and section transitions
- Better balance between dense information and breathing room
- Reusable patterns instead of page-by-page improvisation

## Motion priorities
- Tasteful reveal and transition behavior
- Interaction polish that helps orientation
- Motion should support hierarchy and focus
- Respect reduced-motion preferences
- Avoid excessive motion, gimmicks, and anything that feels ornamental

## What "eye candy" is allowed
- Subtle depth
- Refined hover states
- Smooth section reveals
- Better composition
- More polished transitions
- More premium visual rhythm

## What is not wanted
- Flashy animations for their own sake
- Excessive glassmorphism or decoration
- Visual clutter
- Low-contrast readability issues
- Design choices that fight the content
- A site that feels more like a tech demo than a business website

## Design freedom
Agents may significantly improve layout, section order, components, spacing, typography treatment, surface treatment, motion, and interactions as long as the site remains readable, credible, and easy to edit.
